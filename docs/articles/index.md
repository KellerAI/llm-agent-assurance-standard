## Rendering notes

The four LAAS renderings share the same twelve normative obligations but frame them differently.
The column above reflects the primary framing audience for each article:

- **All** — used when the article addresses the LAAS core premise (action-level assurance, blast
  radius) or multi-agent coordination, concepts that cut across every rendering.
- **SR** — articles whose argument is most legible through model-risk management language
  (SR 11-7 / SR 26-2): effective challenge, ongoing monitoring, vendor accountability, escape-rate
  budgeting as the agent analogue of validation outcomes analysis.
- **IEEE** — articles grounding LAAS in engineering-safety precedent: DO-178C, independent
  verification, enforcement-plane integrity, the Authorized Operating Envelope, and the
  minimal-risk fallback maneuver.
- **ISO** — articles that invoke ISO 14971 harm-severity framing or the ISO/IEC 42001
  intended-use / Operational Design Domain boundary; applies most directly to clinical AI contexts.
- **NIST** — articles mapping to NIST AI RMF functions (GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE):
  observability to enforcement, escape-rate measurement infrastructure, deployer accountability.

**Judgment calls.** The "Rendering(s) supported" column reflects primary audience fit, not
exhaustive coverage — every article is consistent with all four renderings because the underlying
obligations are the same. Articles mapped to "All" (rows 1 and 10) are canonical entry points that
do not privilege one framing. The ISO column is narrow by design: it applies only where a clinical
or intended-use scope argument is load-bearing (rows 7, 18, 24).
